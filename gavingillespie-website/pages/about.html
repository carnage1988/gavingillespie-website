About Me

The best engineers never stop asking why.

I’ve built my career around understanding how technology works, improving systems where I can, and helping others get the best from them.

My Story

I didn’t get into technology because I wanted to work in IT. I got into it because I hated not understanding how things worked.

Growing up, my dad never let me throw broken technology away without first trying to understand why it had failed. If something stopped working, we opened it up, looked for the fault and tried to fix it. Sometimes we fixed it, sometimes we didn’t, but every time we learned something.

That curiosity has stayed with me. Whether it’s a Linux server, a network issue, an Azure migration or a Bash script, I still approach problems the same way: understand the cause, work through the problem methodically and leave the solution better than I found it.

My Journey

I started on busy service desks supporting healthcare and managed service environments, where I learned the importance of communication, documentation and solving problems quickly under pressure.

From there I moved into networking, working directly with customers to deploy new sites, configure routers and troubleshoot connectivity issues.

Today I work as a Technical Consultant supporting enterprise applications, Microsoft technologies, Linux environments and migration projects, while developing automation and documentation that reduce engineering effort.

“Once I get the itch to know something, I have to research it, understand it and be able to talk about it.”

How I Like To Work

When I don’t understand something, I don’t leave it there. I research it, break it, fix it, build it again and then try to explain it clearly.

If I can teach it to someone else, I know I’ve understood it properly. That mindset has helped me move from service desk support into networking, Linux, automation, cloud technologies and infrastructure projects.

Outside Work

Much of what you’ll find on this website started as home projects. My home lab gives me somewhere to experiment with Linux, Docker, networking, Cloudflare, reverse proxies and automation without worrying about breaking production systems.

It’s where ideas become experience. Sometimes those ideas help me improve how I work professionally. Sometimes they simply teach me something new. Both outcomes are worthwhile.

Where I’m Heading

My goal isn’t to know everything. It’s to keep learning.

Over the next few years I’m focusing on infrastructure engineering, automation, cloud technologies and DevOps while continuing to build projects that solve real problems.

Every project on this website is another step along that journey.
